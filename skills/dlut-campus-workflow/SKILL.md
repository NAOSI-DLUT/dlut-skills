# DLUT Campus Workflow

## Goal

Help DLUT students, teachers, and staff turn campus-life or coursework information into practical outputs: checklists, timelines, comparison tables, summaries, drafts, study plans, and document outlines.

## Workflow

1. Identify whether the task is about campus daily life, course learning, documents, data, or local automation.
2. Ask for or inspect the user's source material, such as notices, course requirements, rubrics, files, screenshots, or copied text.
3. Treat deadlines, policies, course rules, and system behavior as time-sensitive. Verify from user-provided or official sources when the answer depends on current facts.
4. Produce the smallest useful artifact for the task: a checklist, table, timeline, outline, summary, script plan, or draft.
5. Highlight assumptions and missing information briefly.

## Quality Rules

- Protect privacy. Do not expose student IDs, phone numbers, account names, grades, or personal schedules unless the user explicitly needs them preserved.
- Respect academic integrity. Help users understand, plan, check, and improve work; do not fabricate data, bypass assignments, or impersonate official sources.
- Do not invent DLUT rules, platform behavior, or course requirements.
- Prefer Chinese output for DLUT campus-facing materials unless the user asks otherwise.
